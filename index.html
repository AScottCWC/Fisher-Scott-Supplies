Expert Advice

Quick garden tips

🌡️

When to start seeds

Most tender seedlings should be started 6–8 weeks before your last frost date. In the UK, this typically means starting seeds indoors from late February through April.

💧

Watering your seedlings

Use a fine mist or water from below to avoid damping off. Keep compost moist but never waterlogged — a good rule is to let the top 5mm dry before watering again.

🐌

Dealing with slugs

Slug pellets are most effective when applied at dusk, thinly scattered around vulnerable plants. Wet evenings are when slugs are most active — perfect timing for protection.

🪴

Potting on

Move seedlings to a larger pot when roots begin to appear from the drainage holes. Going from a 9cm pot to a 2-litre pot gives roots room to develop without over-potting.

☀️

Hardening off

Before moving plants outside permanently, introduce them gradually — start with an hour of outdoor air, increasing over 10–14 days so they adjust to wind, cold, and direct sun.

🌿

Feeding your plants

Once seedlings have their second set of true leaves, begin feeding with a balanced liquid fertiliser every 1–2 weeks. Switch to a high-potash feed once flowering begins.

When should I start sowing seeds in the UK? +

Most tender seeds should be started indoors 6–8 weeks before the last frost. In the UK this typically means sowing from late February through April on a warm windowsill using a heated propagator.

Are Fisher Scott slug pellets safe for wildlife? +

Yes. Our slug killer pellets use a wildlife-friendly formula that is safe around pets, birds and other garden wildlife when applied as directed. Scatter thinly around vulnerable plants at dusk for best results.

What size pot should I move seedlings into after pricking out? +

After pricking out into cell trays, the next step is a 9cm pot. This gives seedling roots room to develop without the risk of over-potting, which can cause waterlogging and slow growth.
